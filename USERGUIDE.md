# Phonograph — User Guide

A simple guide to set up and use Phonograph at
**[phonograph.pravaig.dpdns.org](https://phonograph.pravaig.dpdns.org)**.

## What is Phonograph?

Phonograph keeps track of your music collection across all your devices in one
place. It remembers what songs you have, where each one lives (laptop, phone,
hard drive, etc.), and which copies are the best quality — so you can spot what's
missing or what you could upgrade.

Your actual music files never leave your device. Phonograph only keeps the song
details (title, artist, album, quality, and so on).

## What you can do

- **Catalog each device** as a separate "library" (e.g. *Laptop*, *Phone*, *NAS*).
- **See every song in one list**, automatically combined even if the same song
  is on several devices.
- **Compare devices** to find songs missing from one of them, or lower-quality
  copies you could upgrade.
- **Organize** songs into your own collections; albums and genres are grouped for
  you automatically.
- **Share** a collection with another approved member.
- **Save streaming links** (Spotify, Tidal, Qobuz, Deezer, Amazon Music, YouTube
  Music) on any song.
- **Search, filter, and sort** your whole library.

## Devices you can use

- **To add music (scanning):** a computer (Windows, Mac, or Linux) using
  **Chrome, Edge, or Firefox**. You'll pick a music folder to scan.
- **To browse and manage:** any device — phone, tablet, or computer. The site is
  built to work well on a phone too.

## Getting started

1. **Open** [phonograph.pravaig.dpdns.org](https://phonograph.pravaig.dpdns.org).
2. **Create an account** — choose a username, email, and password.
3. **Wait for approval.** New accounts need an admin to approve them. Once
   approved, sign in and you're in.

## Adding your music (on a computer)

1. Go to **Libraries** and click **New library**. Give it a name for that device
   (for example, *Laptop FLACs*).
2. Open the library and click **Select folder**, then choose your music folder.
3. Phonograph reads the song details from your files (this can take a moment for
   large folders) and shows you a preview.
4. Click **Upload** to save them. Your songs now appear under **Tracks**.

To add music from another device, sign in on that device (or note it down),
create a **new library** for it, and scan its folder the same way.

## Finding your way around

- **Dashboard** — a quick overview, including your quality breakdown.
- **Tracks** — every song. Use the search box, the filters (library, artist,
  collection), and the sort buttons. On the right is an **A–Z bar** — tap or
  swipe it to jump quickly through the list.
- **Collections** — your own playlists plus auto-made Albums and Genres. Create
  one, then add songs to it from a song's page.
- **Analytics** — compare your libraries side by side to see what's missing from
  each, where you have duplicates, and which copies you could upgrade.
- **Sharing** — share a collection with another approved member via a link, then
  find collections shared with you here.

## Tips

- Keep one library per device so comparisons stay clear.
- After you add or remove music on a device, just scan that library again to
  bring it up to date.
- Open any song to edit its details, see all its copies, combine or separate
  versions, and add streaming links.
